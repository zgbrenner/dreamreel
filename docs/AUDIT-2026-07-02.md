# DREAMREEL Audit — 2026-07-02

Owner complaints driving this audit: (1) video never shows up on the site, (2) the output is
muddled with filters/filler — "an old film reel that doesn't work" rather than a dream, (3) clip
selection and blending are poor — title cards surface where they make no narrative/mood sense.

All three trace to a small number of root causes. The biggest is a **data incident in the live
manifest**, not the runtime.

## Root cause #1 — the live manifest hotlinks every visual asset (videos dark since 2026-06-23)

- `v2026.06.23-0359` (last good publish): all 40 videos + 277 images pointed at the R2 CDN.
- `v2026.06.23-1515` (Round 5 audio corpus): **every visual asset's `src` regressed to its origin
  hotlink** (archive.org / iiif.wellcomecollection.org / images.metmuseum.org). Cause:
  `audio/build_corpus.py` was fed the local PRE-publish manifest (`out/manifest.json`, origin
  srcs) instead of the live R2 manifest its docstring promised to augment; `ship_corpus` then
  published it. Every manifest-only reship since (remood, tempo, SemDeDup, aesthetic, textgen,
  SigLIP, shots, entities, sprites, poetry) composed on top of the broken srcs.
- Impact: archive.org is **not CORS-clean** for WebGL (`crossOrigin="anonymous"` — the repo's own
  dev-proxy exists precisely for this), so **every video errors in production** and the reel
  substitutes procedural filler (`fog`/`static`/`horizon`/`orbs`) under the film grade — which is
  literally "an old film reel that doesn't work". The 40 transcoded clips sat unused on R2.
- Side effect: the June-25 `shots[]` work (play an interior shot instead of the film leader) was
  built against the full-length archive.org films — treating a symptom of the regression. Those
  shot offsets (t=20–180 s) are **invalid** for the ~12 s mirrored R2 clips.

**Fixes (this branch):**
- `pipeline/publish/repair_srcs.py` — joins the live manifest against the last-good reference and
  restores all 281 mirrored srcs by asset id; drops the stale full-film `shots[]` from restored
  videos (the R2 clips are already cut at a content-aware interior start, so the
  title-card/leader-at-t=0 problem disappears by construction). Dry-run verified against the real
  bucket: 281/281 repairable, all R2 objects present, output validates against the app's zod loader.
- `publish/upload_r2.publish_manifest` — now **refuses to publish** a manifest whose media srcs
  are external hotlinks (override: `R2_ALLOW_HOTLINKS=1`). This guard would have caught the
  incident at ship time.
- `audio/build_corpus.py` — defaults to fetching the LIVE manifest (like the other reship tools)
  instead of the local pre-publish file.
- Runtime hardening: `VideoPool` clamps a shot window that lies beyond the clip's real duration
  (stale shots froze the clip on its last frame forever); failed/timed-out video elements are torn
  down instead of buffering until GC.

**Deploy status: the repaired manifest is validated but NOT yet shipped** (publishing to the live
`latest.json` needs the owner's go-ahead). To ship, from `pipeline/` with the R2 env set:

```bash
python -m publish.repair_srcs --out out --upload
```

Rollback: re-upload any previous `manifest/manifest.<version>.json` as `manifest/latest.json`.

## Root cause #2 — the wake-mode treatment stack ignored the intensity heartbeat

The 2026 direction is a coherent, lightly-graded baseline with treatment only at escalation. The
intensity signal was already inverted (rest ≈ 0.16), but large parts of the look stack didn't
follow it down:

- The post-FX "dream-event engine" fired a bloom/leak/tint/exposure swell every 4–11 s at full
  amplitude, forever, independent of intensity — now gated on the heartbeat
  (`PostFX.setWakeEnergy`; silent at baseline, full only in surges; classic mode unchanged).
- The dust-speck + scratch overlay ran at half-opacity constantly — now scaled by the heartbeat.
- Wake mode inherited the CLASSIC film defaults for `lightLeak 0.3 / tint 0.25 / breathe 0.5`
  (setParams merges; `baseWakeFilm` never owned them) — now owned at near-off levels.
- Non-focus grade floor at rest was `filmGrade 0.34, chroma 0.19, bloom 0.13` — now
  `0.15 / 0.04 / 0.07`, with the slopes preserved so escalation still grades up.
- The mood-filter catalog had a `0.10` additive strength floor, so a solarize/melt/kaleidoscope
  filter was ALWAYS on despite "identity by default" — the floor is removed (`0.42 × intensity`).
- Surges occupied ~24–30 % of runtime at peak 0.96 — re-tuned to ~10–12 % at peak 0.85
  (`SURGE_MIN_GAP 20→32, MAX_GAP 46→75, DUR 7→5`).

## Root cause #3 — selection/blending: leaps too hot, cards ungated, swaps abrupt

- Non-sequitur leaps hit ~14 % of beats at typical surreality with a near-flat softmax
  (temperature ≈ 0.67), so consecutive picks barely tracked embedding similarity. Re-tuned:
  leap probability is now quadratic in surreality (~4.5 % at 0.5), temperature `0.10 + 0.7·s`,
  drift `0.10 + 0.4·s` — the walk, not random jumps, carries the narrative.
- Title cards fired at ~6–11 % per beat (worst right after a leap) as an opaque held hero with
  mood-affinity-only selection. Now: lower base probability, **never directly after a leap**, and
  a minimum gap of 8 image beats between cards (`CARD_MIN_GAP_BEATS`).
- Wake layer swaps cross-fade over ~0.8 s instead of ~0.3 s (the mood-mapped gl-transition
  catalog remains classic-only — see follow-ups).
- Video-first pool: a video-starved corpus (< 4 clips, e.g. the dev seed with 1) no longer
  collapses the walk to a degenerate single-clip pool; images stay primary until there's enough
  video (`MIN_PRIMARY_VIDEOS`).
- Determinism fix: procedural fallback kinds were drawn from the shared presentation RNG inside
  async load callbacks — network timing could reorder draws and break the same-seed-same-dream
  contract. Now a pure hash of the asset id.
- Stale video pins: a held slot overwritten by a non-video texture kept its pin; now cleared on
  slot reuse.
- Film-clip native audio was silently dead: the `<video>` element stays muted for autoplay, and a
  muted element outputs silence even through `createMediaElementSource`. The mixer now unmutes on
  engage (audibility is still governed by bus gain, ducking, and the master sound toggle).
- `scripts/validate-manifest.ts` crashed outside Vite (`import.meta.env.DEV` deref in
  archiveProxy) — guarded.
